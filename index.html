Serena Viti

The Catholic University of America, Washington, D.C.

Presenting an original research project at the US Conference of Catholic Bishops in Baltimore on the economic impact of the fall of Communism on 28 different countries in Europe, analyzing how the shifting political landscape has affected both the economic welfare and the spiritual development of the region, with a special focus on the growth of the Catholic Church.

She was accepted into the Ropke-Wojtyla Fellowship program hosted by the Ciocca Center for Principled Entrepreneurship at Catholic University. The Fellowship's purpose is to encourage the next generation to critically engage intellectual traditions and authors they might not otherwise encounter, and discuss these writings in a series of colloquia, hosted in D.C.
